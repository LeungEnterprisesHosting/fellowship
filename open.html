Open Campus


An open campus in high school gives students the freedom to make choices and gives students a greater control over their environment

Why Open Campus?

Open campus in high schools is a very controversial topic in American education. Although some educational officials believe that a closed campus is superior, an open campus reaps many benefits. Ultimately, an open campus in high school gives students the freedom to make choices and gives students a greater control over their environment. According to one study, giving students more autonomy improves engagement and enthusiasm in class. One principal who has adopted the programs into his high school believes that most of the students “are responsible and are doing the right things" and that it helps “transition students into adulthood." Another principal said that 95 percent of students use the privilege responsibly.

Benefits

Open education promotes:

  • More individualized instruction
  • Opportunities for independent study
  • Students' acceptance of responsibility for their own educational development

Open campus plans are also a way for high schools to:

  • Develop a workable alternative to traditional high school programs
  • Break down the artificial barriers between the school and the community.
